Research

Our multidisciplinary research group is dedicated to investigating the alterations in metabolism that occur in obesity, fatty liver disease, and cancer. Our goal is to identify biomarkers and therapeutic targets to enhance the treatment and management of these diseases.

Our team is made up of specialists in various fields, including molecular biology, biochemistry, and clinical research. By combining our expertise and utilizing cutting-edge technology, we can gain a comprehensive understanding of the relationship between metabolic dysfunction and the development of obesity and cancer.

Our research focuses on determining the key metabolic pathways and enzymes that are altered in these diseases, and how these changes can be utilized to develop new diagnostic tools and treatments. Our findings have the potential to significantly impact patient care by providing earlier diagnoses and more effective treatments for obesity and cancer.

We are committed to driving progress and improving the lives of those affected by these diseases through our innovative research. We believe that our multidisciplinary approach will lead to a deeper understanding of obesity and cancer and bring us closer to a world where they are manageable and preventable.
